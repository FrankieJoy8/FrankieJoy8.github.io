Manhattan


TAKE A WALK THROUGH CENTRAL PARK

No trip to New York is complete without a trip to Central Park. It is 50 blocks long in the center of Manhattan. You can spend anywhere from a couple hours to a whole day just exploring the park. During the spring, there are paths lined with beautiful flowering bushes. Some highlights include the Great Lawn, the Reservoir and the Central Park Zoo.

VISIT THE ANIMALS IN THE CENTRAL PARK ZOO

The Central Park Zoo is a small zoo located in the South part of Central Park. It will only take a couple hours to visit, and you can enjoy spending time with the snow leopards, bears and sea lions. The petting zoo is also included in your tickets.

TIMES SQUARE

Times Square is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the city. Giant billboards, restaurants and shops surround you as you navigate through the crowds. Going to a Broadway play makes the experience even better.

SPEND THE DAY IN DIFFERNT MUSEUMS

Manhattan has some of the best museums. If you walk up 5th avenue from 80th street, there are plenty of museums to visit. Some of the must-see museums in New York are the Metropolitan Museum of Art (the Met) on 82nd St and 5th ave, the Guggenheim (88th and 5th), MoMA (W 53rd st) and the Natural History Museum (79th and Columbus). It is definitely worth visiting at least one of the museums on your trip to New York.

TAKE A LOOK AT SOME OF THE WORLD FAMOUS BUILDINGS

One of the most touristy things to do is visiting some of the tallest and most famous buildings in New York. The Empire State Building - built in 1931, is 102 stories high. At the time of its completion, it was the tallest building in the world - surpassing the Chrysler Building. The Chrysler - 1,046ft tall - was built around same time as the Empire State. It was completed in 1930, and was the tallest building in the world for 11 months (until the Empire State Building was completed). One World Trait Center (the Freedom Tower) is a more recent building and was completed in 2015. The 1,776ft tall building is a memorial for the people who died in the 9/11 attacks on the previous World Trait Centers.

TAKE THE FERRY OVER TO LIBERTY ISLAND

This symbol for freedom and hope of one of the most famous statues in the country. A gift from France in 1886, it is a robed woman (representing Libertas, a Roman liberty goddess) holding a book labeled: July IV MDCCLXXVI. When immigrants were coming into this country, this was the first thing they saw. Tickets to the statue (and Ellis Island) can be purchased in advance online. The ferry leaves from Battery Park.

WALK THE HIGHLINE

The Highline is a 1.45 mile long, elevated garden made from old railroad tracks. The garden is a unique example of how old things can be repurposed into something interesting, beautiful and modern.
